Local SEO strategies are geared towards businesses that serve a specific geographical location. These businesses usually have a physical presence and rely on people from the local area to become customers. National or global SEO strategies, on the other hand, target a broader audience without necessarily considering a geographic location. The main difference between the two is the level of specificity.

A major benefit of local SEO is that it can drive more foot traffic to physical locations, as well as promote online sales within a particular region. Approximately 46% of all searches on Google seek local information (Source: GoGulf), making it essential for any business operating in a specific area to focus its marketing efforts locally.

One effective way to improve local SEO is by optimizing your Google My Business (GMB) account, which has been reported to impact around 25% of your website’s ability to rank locally (Source: Moz). Key components of GMB optimization include ensuring that your business name, address and phone number (NAP) are accurate and consistent across all platforms, creating clear business descriptions with relevant keywords and customer reviews.

In contrast, national or global SEO strategies will generally focus on broader search terms that cover large audiences. This means targeting high traffic potential keywords that can attract users regardless of their physical location. Ranking nationally also typically requires greater investment in content marketing, link building and social media outreach than local SEO does.

Ultimately, both national and local SEO have their place in online marketing strategies and will depend on the individual goals of each business owner. If you're looking to drive traffic to your brick-and-mortar store or service area specific business then focusing on Local SEO would be ideal. If you’re trying to increase brand awareness or sell products nationally or internationally then National SEO should be considered.
